## 🌟 STORY OF THE DAY

**Florida's Criminal Investigation into OpenAI Marks New Enforcement Era**

Florida Attorney General James Uthmeier announced criminal subpoenas to OpenAI and ChatGPT, launching what appears to be the first criminal investigation by a U.S. state into an AI company. While details of the alleged harms remain limited, the investigation signals that state attorneys general are willing to use criminal enforcement tools — not just civil lawsuits or regulatory actions — against AI companies.

This matters because it creates asymmetric legal risk. OpenAI and other labs have prepared for FTC investigations, EU regulations, and class action lawsuits. Criminal investigations operate differently: they carry potential for individual liability, they command different legal resources, and they create reputational damage that civil matters do not.

The timing — amid federal efforts to preempt state AI laws — creates a contradictory landscape. Washington may block state *regulations*, but it cannot easily stop state *investigations*. Expect other attorneys general to watch Florida's approach closely.
