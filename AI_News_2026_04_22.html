## 🌟 STORY OF THE DAY

**India Signs Pax Silica Declaration — Reshaping Global AI Alliances**

At the India AI Impact Summit, India formally joined the US-led Pax Silica Declaration, a strategic alliance aimed at building resilient supply chains for critical minerals, semiconductors, and AI infrastructure. Backed by 88 countries, this marks the most significant realignment of technology geopolitics since the CHIPS Act.

Here's why this matters: AI development depends on three chokepoints — advanced chips (dominated by Taiwan/US), rare earth minerals (dominated by China), and massive data centers (dominated by US tech giants). The Pax Silica framework attempts to create alternative supply chains that reduce dependence on any single nation.

For India specifically, this opens doors to semiconductor manufacturing partnerships, critical mineral access, and potentially preferential treatment for AI infrastructure investments. But it also locks India into a US-aligned technology bloc at a moment when maintaining strategic autonomy has been a stated priority.

The summit generated billions in investment pledges, but the real test comes in execution. As analysts noted, US tech giants alone plan to spend over $630 billion on AI globally this year — making India's announced totals look modest by comparison. The question isn't whether India has a seat at the table. It's whether India can convert diplomatic wins into manufacturing capacity, talent pipelines, and homegrown AI capabilities.
